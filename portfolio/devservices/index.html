"How do I know if Tetyana Fytsyak is right for my project?"

Not every developer is right for every project. Some on stand-alone utility apps, others on cloud services.

Some contractors bid on every job that crosses their desk, regardless of that all important "fit" factor.

With Anatoly, I take a different approach. I believe the easiest path to happy clients is to take on projects that I know I can do a better job with than anyone else, and refer you to a colleague if I feel they are who you really need instead.

I turn down a surprising amount of work with this approach, but I have a 100% "I would hire you again in a heartbeat!" success rate with clients as a result.

Consider the needs of your app...

  • Do I need a shared server solution, with my users able to share data with each other?
  • Do I need maps, GPS, notifications, animations and other aspects of a rich user experience?
  • Is social media and ability to connect with other services important for my app?
  • Do you want an app that uses the latest mobile features and keeps current with new releases?
  • Is it important to you that your app be able to run on both iOS, Android and Windows Phone(now or in the future)?
  • Do I need help and recommendations for providing "back end" services for my apps?
  • Would it be beneficial to use in-app advertising or in-app purchases in my app?

If you answered "Yes" to many of those questions, I am probably a very good fit for your needs.
I find that weeks of coding and testing can save me hours of planning!

Software development is a complex process. Would you rather pay your developer for those hours of planning, or for the weeks of coding and testing to recover from focusing on the sale rather than the product?
